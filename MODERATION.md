# Moderation and Correction Process

1. A report identifies the content, concern, evidence, and requested remedy.
2. A maintainer acknowledges it and checks for immediate safety, privacy, or legal risk.
3. The content is reviewed under the relevant policy, with temporary limits only when necessary.
4. The decision and rationale are recorded at an appropriate level of detail.
5. The contributor may appeal with new evidence or a process objection.

Good-faith disagreement is not itself a moderation violation. Harassment, threats, doxxing, plagiarism, fabricated citations, and coordinated manipulation are.
