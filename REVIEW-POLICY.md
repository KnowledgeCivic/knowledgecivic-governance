# Contribution and Review Policy

## Review levels

- Level 1: spelling, links, formatting, and accessibility fixes.
- Level 2: ordinary explanatory changes requiring one independent review.
- Level 3: sensitive, high-impact, legal, medical, safety, or politically contested material requiring two reviewers and an explicit limitations section.

## Evidence requirements

Use primary sources where feasible. Record publication date, access date, jurisdiction, and relevant methodology. Do not cite a search-result snippet as evidence.

## Decision labels

`established`, `supported`, `contested`, `uncertain`, and `proposal` describe the status of a claim, not the worth of a person or viewpoint.

## Corrections

Corrections should preserve the history of material changes. A correction note states what changed, why, when, and which source supports it.
